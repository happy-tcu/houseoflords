04 · IPDA Impromptu · 1 vs 1

1 vs 1. Five motions. You strike them down.

Every round hands your room five motions. You don’t pick — you cancel.

1
Opp strikes first. Cancels one motion.
2
Prop strikes next. Cancels one.
3
Alternate until 4 are gone.
4
The one that remains is your debate motion.

Scoring
06 · How judges decide

Four axes. One winner.

Every speaker gets scored on 4 things, 5 points each. Then the judge picks a winner — not always the higher score, but the one who argued the round better.

01
Argument
Clarity of claim, logic, evidence.
/ 5
02
Rebuttal & CX
Direct engagement, quality of Q&A.
/ 5
03
Delivery
Pace, clarity, presence, use of time.
/ 5
04
Persuasion
Would you vote for their side?
/ 5

Tiebreakers: wins first, then total points. Top 4 across the prelims advance to the quarters. Quarter winners advance to the final by wins + speaker points.

Prep
07 · What to actually do in 30 minutes

Your 30-min prep recipe.

1
2 min · Read the motion aloud. Define every ambiguous word before you argue about it.
2
5 min · Three arguments, one line each. If you can't fit an argument in one sentence, you don't understand it yet.
3
5 min · One story per argument. A Rwanda-specific example. Not a study, not a stat — a story.
4
3 min · Predict their three attacks. Write your first-line rebuttal for each.
5
15 min · Rehearse the constructive out loud. Not in your head. Time yourself. Land the last line.
Silence in your head ≠ silence on the floorSlide 8 / 11
In the room
08 · During your speech + CX

When it's your turn to speak.

While you're speaking
  • Signpost. Say “my first argument… my second… my third.” Judges write faster than they think.
  • One point per minute. Anything more and nobody remembers.
  • Land the line. Your last sentence is what the judge writes down.
In cross-examination
  • Short questions. If your question has “and” in it, cut it in half.
  • Don’t argue in the question — use their answer.
  • Stay standing, stay steady. CX rewards composure, not volume.

Don’ts
09 · What loses rounds on day one

These lose rounds. Every time.

1
Reading a full script. Judges score presence. Notes are fine, scripts kill you.
2
Apologizing. “I’m not sure but…” “This is just an idea…” Delete these from your vocabulary Saturday.
3
Running out mid-sentence at STOP. Time your closing to land at 0:15, not 0:00.
4
Ignoring their strongest argument. Judges notice. Address it, even if briefly.
Presence > polish · answer > ignoreSlide 10 / 11
